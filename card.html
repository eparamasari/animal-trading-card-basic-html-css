Giraffe

A giraffe can run as fast as 55 km an hour over short distances, or cruise at 15 km/h over longer distances.

  • Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis
  • Average Height: 4.6 – 6.1 m
  • Average Weight: 828 (f) – 1,192 (m) kg
  • Average Lifespan: 25 years (in the wild)
  • Habitat: savanna, grassland
  • Diet: herbivore (incl. Acacia)

Giraffes are native to sub-Saharan Africa and they live in habitats where the available food varies throughout the year. An artiodactyl mammal, it is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. It is traditionally considered to be one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies.
